### 2 — The stakes
**The answer is the new shortlist.**
When someone asks an assistant what to buy, they don't get ten blue links to
weigh up. They get three names. You're on that list or you're invisible — and
there's no page two to climb.

### 3 — The wound
**Knowing you're invisible doesn't make you visible.**
Every tool in this category will show you a number of "opportunities" and wish
you luck. Nothing `[R]` checks whether you did the work. Nothing tells you
whether it worked. Ninety days later the dashboard is a tab nobody opens.
HeyOtis is built the other way round. The measurement exists to serve the loop
— not the other way round.

### 4 — The loop (core)
**A loop that closes itself.**
1. **Measure — see exactly how you show up.** Every assistant, every query in
   your campaign. `[R]` Where you appear in the answer, how you're described,
   and who gets cited instead of you.
2. **Diagnose — find the reason, with evidence.** Detectors read your surfaces
   and the answers, and `[R]` every finding traces to a fact you can click: the
   answer, the citation, the page, the date.
3. **Prioritise — get the short list that matters.** Ranked by impact and
   effort, scoped to a campaign, `[R]` sized to get done. If we can't verify
   it, we don't recommend it.
4. **Verify — we watch the work land.** The engine re-checks your site until
   the change is `[R]` live. Nobody has to tell us. If it slips later, you'll
   know that too.
5. **Prove — measure the lift.** Visibility, referrals and conversions, tied
   back `[R]` to the move that earned them.

**And then it compounds.**
Most tools reset every month. HeyOtis gets sharper: `[R]` every move it proves
— or disproves — teaches it what to recommend next, for your brand and your
category.

### 5 — The honesty wedge
**We'd rather tell you nothing than tell you wrong.**
Confident, wrong advice is the cheapest thing an AI product can make — and the
most expensive thing you can act on. So we made it hard to produce.
- The AI never does the maths. Every number you see is computed by
  deterministic code. The model writes the words; it never counts. It cannot
  invent a statistic, because it is never `[R]` asked to produce one.
- If we can't measure it, we won't suggest it. A `[R]` recommendation we can't
  verify is just a polite opinion. Those get suppressed before they reach you.
- Every claim has a receipt. Click any finding and see the evidence it came
  from, and the date it was true.
- New detection ships dark. Every new pattern the engine learns to spot is
  reviewed by a human before a single brand sees it.
